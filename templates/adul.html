Toor Dal is one of the most commonly consumed lentils in India and is prone to adulteration with cheaper and potentially harmful substitutes like Khesari Dal. Adulteration of Toor Dal with Khesari Dal is a serious public health concern, as it contains a neurotoxin called ODAP (β-N-oxalyl-L-α,β-diaminopropionic acid) that can cause paralysis and other serious health issues if consumed in large amounts.

Toor dal adulteration is primarily motivated by financial incentives, lack of regulations, and inefficient enforcement. Businesses seek to reduce costs, increase profits and gain a competitive advantage through adulteration. Establishing quality requirements and enforcing penalties can deter adulteration. It is important to regularly inspect producers and ensure they follow regulations. Polluted toor dal poses health risks such as cancer and paralysis. Setting minimum pricing, regulatory frameworks and enforcement can guarantee customers receive high-quality toor dal.
